Forensic Career Path

Career Overview

What Do They Do?

  • Collect, identify, classify, and analyze physical evidence related to criminal investigations.
  • Perform tests on weapons or substances, such as fiber, hair, and tissue to determine significance to investigation.
  • May testify as expert witnesses on evidence or crime laboratory techniques.
  • Use photographic or video equipment to document evidence or crime scenes

Forensic technicians are essential to the criminal justice system, utilizing scientific methods to analyze physical evidence from crime scenes. They typically hold a bachelor's degree in forensic science or a related field and receive extensive on-the-job training. Their responsibilities include crime scene investigation, laboratory analysis, report writing, and testifying in court. Key skills for forensic technicians include attention to detail, technical proficiency, critical thinking, and strong communication abilities. The demand for forensic technicians is expected to grow due to advancements in forensic technology and an increased reliance on scientific evidence in criminal investigations, making this a promising career with a positive job outlook.
